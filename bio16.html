Joseph Stalin

Joseph Stalin, born on December 18, 1878, in Gori, Georgia, was a Soviet revolutionary, politician, and dictator who served as the leader of the Soviet Union from the mid-1920s until his death in 1953. Stalin played a central role in the establishment of the Soviet state and the transformation of the Soviet Union into a major world power. Stalin rose to prominence within the Bolshevik Party under the leadership of Vladimir Lenin, and following Lenin's death in 1924, he emerged as the preeminent figure in Soviet politics. Over the course of his leadership, Stalin implemented a series of radical economic, social, and political reforms aimed at modernizing the Soviet Union and consolidating his power.
