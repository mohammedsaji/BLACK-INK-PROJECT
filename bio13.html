Neil Armstrong

Neil Armstrong, born on August 5, 1930, in Wapakoneta, Ohio, United States, was an American astronaut, aerospace engineer, naval aviator, and the first person to walk on the Moon. Armstrong's historic steps onto the lunar surface on July 20, 1969, during NASA's Apollo 11 mission, marked a defining moment in human history and solidified his place as one of the most iconic figures of the 20th century. Before his legendary lunar landing, Armstrong served as a naval aviator and test pilot, accumulating thousands of hours of flight time and honing his skills as a pilot. In 1962, he joined NASA as part of the second group of astronauts selected for the Gemini and Apollo programs.
