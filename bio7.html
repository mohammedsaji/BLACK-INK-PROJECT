Henry Ford

Henry Ford, born on July 30, 1863, in Greenfield Township, Michigan, United States, was an American industrialist and the founder of the Ford Motor Company. He is credited with revolutionizing the automotive industry by introducing assembly line manufacturing techniques and making automobiles affordable and accessible to the masses. Ford's groundbreaking innovations in mass production and efficiency transformed the way automobiles were manufactured, leading to the widespread adoption of assembly line methods in manufacturing industries around the world. His introduction of the Model T, launched in 1908, became one of the most influential and iconic vehicles in automotive history, making car ownership a reality for millions of Americans.
