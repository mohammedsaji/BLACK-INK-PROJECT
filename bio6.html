Abdul Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, was an eminent Indian scientist, aerospace engineer, and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He is widely regarded as the "Missile Man of India" for his pivotal role in India's missile development program and his contributions to the advancement of science and technology in the country. Dr. Kalam's career in science and technology began in the 1960s when he joined India's Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO). He played a key role in the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle and was instrumental in the design and development of various missile systems, including the Agni and Prithvi missiles.
