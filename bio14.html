Tipu Sultan

Tipu Sultan, born on November 20, 1750, in Devanahalli, present-day Karnataka, India, was a prominent ruler of the Kingdom of Mysore in southern India. He was the eldest son of Sultan Hyder Ali, and he succeeded his father as the ruler of Mysore in 1782. Tipu Sultan is often remembered for his resistance against British colonial expansion in India during the late 18th century. He waged several wars against the British East India Company, seeking to preserve Mysorean sovereignty and resist British encroachment on his kingdom's territory.
