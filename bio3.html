Charles Babbage

Charles Babbage, often referred to as the "Father of the Computer," was a British mathematician, philosopher, inventor, and mechanical engineer who lived in the 19th century. His work laid the foundation for modern computing and digital technology. Babbage was born on December 26, 1791, in London, England, and he passed away on October 18, 1871. Babbage is best known for conceptualizing and designing the first mechanical general-purpose computer, known as the "Difference Engine." He conceived this machine in the early 19th century to automate the process of calculating mathematical tables, which were prone to errors when computed by hand. Although Babbage never completed the construction of the Difference Engine during his lifetime due to technical and financial challenges, his designs and ideas were revolutionary and ahead of their time.
